Frequently Asked

Questions we hear early.

Q1When should we bring a general contractor into the project?
As early as possible — ideally during design. Preconstruction is where budget, constructability, and schedule risk are actually managed. Bringing us in at the drawing stage almost always costs less than fixing surprises during construction.
Q2What size projects does Meridian take on?
We specialize in commercial interiors at scale — from focused tenant improvements to multi-floor headquarters and technically complex lab and healthcare builds. If it's an interior environment that has to perform, it's in our wheelhouse.
Q3Do you self-perform any of the work?
We self-perform select scopes where it protects quality and schedule, and partner with a vetted network of specialty subcontractors for the rest. Every trade partner is held to the same safety and craft standard we hold ourselves to.
Q4Which industries do you focus on?
Five core sectors: commercial office, science & technology, healthcare, hospitality, and industrial & manufacturing. Deep repetition in these markets is exactly what lets us move quickly on complex work.
Q5What does being employee-owned mean for my project?
The person managing your job is an owner of the company, not just an employee of it. That shows up as transparency on budget, accountability on schedule, and a long-term view of the relationship rather than a single transaction.
Q6How do subcontractors get on your bid list?
We're always expanding our trade partner network. Reach out through the contact section below and our preconstruction team will get you into our prequalification process.
